TSB (Training Stress Balance)

Formula: CTL - ATL

Target: -10 to +10 (optimal training zone)

Meaning: Your form/freshness level. Negative = fatigued but adapting, Positive = fresh/recovered.

  • +10 to +25 Fresh, race-ready
  • -10 to +10 Optimal training zone
  • -25 to -10 Productive overload
  • < -25 Overreached, need recovery
CTL (Chronic Training Load)

Formula: 42-day exponential weighted moving average of TSS

Target: Gradual increase (4-8 CTL/week)

Meaning: Your fitness level. Higher = more fit, but takes weeks to build.

ATL (Acute Training Load)

Formula: 7-day exponential weighted moving average of TSS

Target: Should track with CTL

Meaning: Your fatigue level. Responds quickly to recent training.

HRV (Heart Rate Variability)

Target: 95-105% of baseline

Meaning: Recovery indicator. Lower = more stress/fatigue. Track morning resting HRV.

Ramp Rate

Formula: CTL change over 7 days

Target: +4 to +8 CTL/week

Meaning: How fast you're building fitness. Too fast = injury risk, too slow = no adaptation.

Durability Index

Target: < 8% drift

Meaning: How well you maintain power/pace as workout progresses. Lower drift = better endurance.

ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio)

Formula: ATL / CTL (7-day / 42-day load)

Target: 0.8 to 1.2

Meaning: Training load balance. Outside range = injury risk increases.

Recovery Index

Formula: (HRV/baseline) × ((TSB+25)/25)

Target: ≥ 1.0

Meaning: Combined recovery score. Factors in both HRV and training stress balance.

Status Definitions
  • ON TARGET: All metrics in optimal range, continue current plan
  • OVERREACHED: High fatigue, consider easy week or rest day
  • UNDERTRAINED: Low load, can increase training volume
  • RECOVERY REQUIRED: Multiple metrics in red zone, mandatory rest needed
